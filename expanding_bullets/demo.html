The House is set on Thursday to give strong bipartisan support to a doc fix deal negotiated between John Boehner and Nancy Pelosi.

  • The "doc fix" is a term for the annual prevention of scheduled cuts in Medicare fees to doctors.
    • A law was passed in the 1990s that sought to restrain costs by tying doctors' pay to a formula--a formal that most agree makes little sense.
    • Since then, doc fixes have been used as short-term solutions, keeping doctors' payments stable and occasionally giving them a small raise.
  • This "permanent doc fix" would allow for regular doctor fee increases.
    • Some wealthier Medicare beneficiaries would pay more, and some of the cost would be added to the deficit.
    • This permanent doc fix would also extend a children's health program and some other health initiatives.
  • This is a major moment for Mr Boehner.
    • He is being praised by Republicans and Democrats, both inside and outside of Congress, for stepping up to strike a bargain with Democrats rather than trying to satisfy more conservative Republicans who have not been eager to compromise.
    • This could be a template for future legislative deals if this one is successful without undermining Mr. Boehner among his rank and file.
    • He is already taking some hits from his right.
  • Both Democrats and Republicans would like to run up the vote count as high as possible to put more pressure on the Senate.
    • The deal has encountered resistance from Democrats trying to squeeze more out of the deal.
